Conference Overview

Across the world, young people are reshaping democracy—from climate protests and electoral reform to digital rights and grassroots resistance. Yet despite their energy and numbers, youth remain underrepresented in parliaments, policy spaces, and international forums. The Youth & Democracy Conference challenges this status quo.

Hosted at Marwadi University by the Centre for Youth Policy (CYP) and the Young India Foundation (YIF), this two-day international convening brings together youth leaders, scholars, policymakers, and practitioners from the Global South and beyond to examine how young people engage with democratic structures and how democracies must adapt to fully include, represent, and empower them.

Designed as academic in form and participatory in spirit, the conference pairs daytime paper presentations, research panels, and closed-door policy roundtables with evening keynotes, cultural showcases, town halls, and intergenerational dialogues that connect evidence with public imagination.
